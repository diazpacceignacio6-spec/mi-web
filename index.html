My Story

From disassembling radios to designing production systems

A self-made path into engineering — no technical family background, just curiosity that kept compounding.

01 · Curiosity

Taking things apart

As a kid I was constantly disassembling radios, remotes and DVD players; modifying software on PC and mobile; building things like a bike light from LEDs and salvaged parts. No engineers at home — my father is a dentist, my mother a teacher — just a pull I couldn't ignore.

02 · Decision

Electronics, by design

A vocational test pointed me to electronics, and the match stuck. I chose UNNE in Corrientes mostly for proximity — 220 km from home — but what I'd find there reshaped how I see engineering.

03 · Formation

Systems over circuits

A young Electrotechnics II professor flipped my view: engineering wasn't about components, it was about systems — flows, dependencies, feedback loops. That mindset carried me through Bioinstrumentation, DSP and the hard jump from Goya to Buenos Aires.

04 · Today

Building fluent systems

I design products and the processes that ship them. Obsessed with internal excellence as a driver of external performance, operational clarity, and making technology accessible — in tools, in organizations, in education.
